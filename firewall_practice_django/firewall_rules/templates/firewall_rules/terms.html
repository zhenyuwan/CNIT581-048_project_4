Private A/B

Private A/B are machines that are inside the firewall. They will have private addresses, 10.0.0.0 - 10.255.255.255; 172.16.0.0 - 172.31.255.255; 192.168.0.0 - 192.168.255.255

External Machine

External Machines are those on the "Internet." They will have any address not in the reserved private address range.

Firewall

The firewall in this tool has limited functions. It can filter traffic and act like a simple router. But beware, firewalls in the real world are more capable than what appears in this tool.

Subnet

The subnet is what makes networks today possible, without which we would only have a giant internet.

LAN

LAN refers to Local Area Network. Purdue CIT network, for example, is a LAN network.

WAN

WAN refers to Wide Area Network. Internet is one example of WAN.

DMZ

A DMZ is where an enterprise can put dangerous out-facing services on. Examples of those services include HTTP, SMB, and MySQL

Direction

The direction of the firewall rule can determine the source and destination address. If the firewall rule is applied on the WAN interface, and the direction is IN, the source address will be the address of the public machine while the destination address will be the address of the private machine

Source Address

Source address refers to where the traffic comes from

Destination Address

Destination address refers to where the traffic is going to
